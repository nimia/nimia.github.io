I am a PhD candidate in the department of EE (though I'm mostly doing CS-stuff) at Tel-Aviv University, under the supervision of prof. Yuval Shavitt.

My main research interest is making the Internet faster and more secure. I especially love everything related to TCP congestion control.


My last completed project is a fast implementation of Dijkstra's algorithm. As far as I can tell, this implementation is very fast indeed, comparing favorably to the Boost implementation (and I don't have to tell you, the boost guys know what they're doing!).

The code is available here, though be warned, currently everything except the actual algorithm implementation is not what I consider ready for public use. Loading graphs and storing the results is especially problematic at the moment. I will get around to putting something reasonable in the future, in the mean time you can always email me if you want to use the code, I'll be happy to help.

A paper about this should be making the rounds somewhere.


Many years ago, I was a co-author, with Iftach Ragoler and Yossi Matias, of a paper which was presented in ADHOC-NOW 2004:

Ragoler, Iftach, Yossi Matias, and Nimrod Aviram. "Adaptive Probing and Communication in Sensor Networks." Ad-Hoc, Mobile, and Wireless Networks: Third International Conference, ADHOC-NOW 2004, Vancouver, Canada, July 22-24, 2004, Proceedings. Vol. 3158. Springer, 2004.
